On First Days

January 21, 2026

I've always been excited for the First Day of something. First days are amazing. They can produce a lot of emotions in you. Happiness, fear, anxiety, joy, interest. A first day makes you clean your room, iron your clothes, cut your hair. It makes you reflect on the future and embrace the present.

In life, we'll have tens of first days, and it's important you pay attention to the details. How you feel, what you expect, what really happens. Laugh if you want, cry if you feel the need. Everything is allowed.

On a good first day, you'll meet new people, find a new hobby, taste delicious food, ignite a spark of curiosity in your brain. It could define your entire life if all the variables appear in the right proportions.

In contrast, a bad first day scares the shit out of you. These will come after you lose someone you love, when a project fails or if you are badly injured. Normally a bad first day challenges the conception of who you are. And it forces you to learn how to heal.

Regardless of whether it is a good or bad one, embrace it. Take your time to assimilate their arrival and don't feel bad if they catch you off guard. Because that is another of its components. The spontaneity.
